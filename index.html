Our School Story

A journey of excellence, growth, and community impact

From Humble Beginnings to Educational Excellence

In the heart of our community, Worabe Secondary School began its journey in 1985 with just three classrooms and a vision to provide quality education to local children. What started as a small initiative has now grown into one of the region's most respected educational institutions.

Our founders, a group of dedicated educators and community leaders, believed that every child deserved access to quality education regardless of their background. They pooled their resources, secured a small plot of land, and with the help of volunteers, constructed the first classrooms. The school opened its doors to 47 students in its inaugural year.

Over the decades, we've witnessed countless success stories - students who arrived with dreams and left with the tools to achieve them. Our alumni now serve as doctors, engineers, teachers, and community leaders across the country and beyond. Many return to share their experiences and mentor current students, creating a cycle of inspiration and support.

The school's expansion in 2005 marked a significant milestone, adding science laboratories, a library, and sports facilities. This growth was made possible through the dedication of our staff, support from the community, and partnerships with educational organizations. We've continued to upgrade our facilities, incorporating technology into our classrooms while maintaining our commitment to personalized education.

Our curriculum has evolved to meet the changing needs of students and society. While maintaining strong foundations in core subjects, we've introduced programs in technology, entrepreneurship, and environmental studies. Our extracurricular activities include sports, arts, debate clubs, and community service projects, ensuring students develop holistically.

Today, Worabe Secondary stands as a beacon of hope and opportunity, continuing our founders' mission to empower young minds through education. We remain committed to our core values while adapting to the changing educational landscape. Our teachers participate in ongoing professional development, and we maintain small class sizes to ensure each student receives individual attention.

Our Guiding Philosophy

We believe education extends beyond textbooks - it's about nurturing curious minds, building character, and preparing students for life's challenges. Our holistic approach balances academic rigor with personal development. We emphasize critical thinking, creativity, and collaboration, skills essential for success in the 21st century.

Community Impact

Beyond our campus, we actively engage with the local community through outreach programs, adult education classes, and environmental initiatives, reinforcing our role as a cornerstone of community development. Our students participate in community service projects, learning the value of civic responsibility while making tangible contributions to our neighborhood.

Looking to the Future

As we look ahead, we're excited about plans to further expand our facilities, introduce new technology programs, and strengthen our international partnerships. Our vision is to become a model for secondary education in our region, combining academic excellence with character development and community engagement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Worabe Secondary School

What are the admission requirements for Worabe Secondary School?

Admission to Worabe Secondary School requires completion of primary education with satisfactory academic performance. Applicants must submit their primary school transcripts, a completed application form, and attend an entrance examination. We also consider extracurricular activities and personal interviews for final selection.

What is the school's academic calendar?

Our academic year runs from September to June, divided into two semesters. The first semester runs from September to January, followed by a short break, and the second semester from February to June. We observe all national holidays and have breaks for Christmas, Easter, and summer vacation.

Does the school offer boarding facilities?

Yes, we offer boarding facilities for both male and female students. Our boarding houses are supervised by experienced house parents and provide a safe, supportive environment. Facilities include comfortable dormitories, study areas, recreational spaces, and nutritious meals prepared in our school kitchen.

What extracurricular activities are available?

We offer a wide range of extracurricular activities including sports (football, basketball, athletics), arts (music, drama, visual arts), academic clubs (science club, debate team, math club), and community service programs. Students are encouraged to participate in at least one activity to develop their talents and interests.

How does the school support students with learning difficulties?

We have a dedicated learning support department that provides individualized assistance to students with learning difficulties. This includes specialized tutoring, modified learning materials, and accommodations during assessments. Our teachers are trained to identify and support diverse learning needs in the classroom.

What career guidance services are available?

Our career guidance program includes individual counseling, career assessment tests, university and college information sessions, and guidance on course selection. We also organize career fairs and invite professionals from various fields to share their experiences with students.

Are there scholarship opportunities available?

Yes, we offer scholarships based on academic merit, financial need, and special talents. These include full and partial scholarships. Applications are reviewed annually, and students must maintain certain academic standards to continue receiving scholarship support.

How can parents get involved in school activities?

Parents are encouraged to join our Parent-Teacher Association (PTA), attend school events, volunteer for activities, and participate in parent-teacher conferences. We also welcome parent input through surveys and suggestion boxes to continuously improve our school community.
